Team Name Generator

Every custom team needs a name. Roll dice against the City and Nickname pools below, or pick manually. Combine them to create your franchise.

How It Works

A team name is built from two parts: a City and a Nickname. Roll once on each pool, combine the results, then create a 2–3 letter abbreviation using the initials.

Example: Roll 1d35 → result 18 → Lakefield. Roll 1d45 → result 47 → Wolves. Team name: Lakefield Wolves (LW).
Abbreviation Rule: Use the first letter of each word. Two-word names = 2 letters (LW). Three-word names = 3 letters (e.g. "New Cascade Storm" → NCS). Keep abbreviations unique within your league.

Section 02

The Rating System

Every Quick Play Football team is defined by three numbers. These ratings determine how often a team scores, how well they suppress opponents, and how they perform in close games.

The Three Stats

Stat Range What It Does High = Better?
OFF (Offense) 4 – 21 Added to opponent's DEF to determine your team's Class for each matchup. Higher OFF means a better class and more scoring potential. Yes
DEF (Defense) 4 – 21 Used as the opponent's modifier. Your DEF is subtracted when working out the opponent's class. Higher DEF suppresses opposing offenses. Yes
CGF (Close Game Factor) 0 – 12 Applied when the final margin is ≤8 points. Modifies the trailing team's last-possession roll. Higher CGF = better clutch performance. Yes
How Class is Calculated: Your Class for a given game = your OFF + opponent's DEF + home/away modifier (home: +2, away: −2, neutral: 0). Look up the total on the Class Threshold table below.

The Class System

Class is the in-game tier derived from your matchup total. It determines which row of the Scoring Chart you use each possession.

Class Total Points (OFF + Opp DEF + modifier) Description
AA39 or moreElite — dominant scorers, high ceiling
A34 – 38Strong — above average, consistent scoring
B29 – 33Average — competitive, balanced output
C25 – 28Below average — workable but limited
D22 – 24Weak — struggles offensively
E19 – 21Poor — low scoring ceilings
F16 – 18Very poor — frequent shutout results
G12 – 15Basement level — minimal scoring
H7 – 11Bottom tier — rare scores only
Z6 or lessRock bottom — almost no scoring chances

Worked Example

Team A (Lakefield Wolves): OFF 16, DEF 13, CGF 8
Team B (Riverside Storm): OFF 11, DEF 15, CGF 5

Wolves' class (home): Wolves OFF (16) + Storm DEF (15) + home (2) = 33 → Class B
Storm's class (away): Storm OFF (11) + Wolves DEF (13) − away (2) = 22 → Class D

Wolves use the B row on the Scoring Chart each possession. Storm uses the D row.

Section 03

Team Tiers & Stat Rolling

When creating a custom team, you first assign a Tier — which sets the team's competitive level. Then roll dice to generate the exact stats within that tier's range.

The Five Tiers

Tier OFF Mod DEF Mod CGF Mod Description
Elite +9+9+6 Championship contenders. Top of the league, perennial favourites.
Strong +6+6+4 Playoff-calibre. Consistent, competitive, dangerous in the right matchup.
Average +3+3+2 .500 teams. Capable of beating anyone, but won't dominate.
Weak +0+0+1 Rebuilding. Gets outclassed often, but has occasional bright spots.
Basement −2−2+0 Bottom dwellers. Rare wins, limited scoring, no CGF help.

Rolling Stats

Each stat is rolled separately with the following dice, then the tier modifier is added:

Stat Roll Add Modifier Clamp Range Notes
OFF 2d6 + tier OFF mod 4 – 21 Bell curve: 7 is most likely result on 2d6
DEF 2d6 + tier DEF mod 4 – 21 Rolled independently — OFF and DEF can differ
CGF 1d6 + tier CGF mod 0 – 12 Single die — less variance, more predictable
Clamping: If the roll + modifier falls outside the valid range, clamp to the nearest limit. Example: Elite team rolls 2 on 2d6 for OFF (2 + 9 = 11) — valid. Rolls 12 (12 + 9 = 21) — clamped to 21. Basement rolls 2 (2 − 2 = 0) — clamped to 4.

Conference & Division Setup

For leagues of 8+ teams, consider organising into conferences and divisions. Suggested structures:

League Size Suggested Structure Notes
8 teams2 conferences × 4 teamsTop 2 per conference make playoffs (4-team bracket)
12 teams2 conferences × 2 divisions × 3 teamsDivision winners + 2 wild cards
16 teams2 conferences × 2 divisions × 4 teamsStandard NFL-style structure
32 teams2 conferences × 4 divisions × 4 teamsFull NFL mirror — 12-team playoff bracket

Balancing Tips

Tip 1 — Spread tiers across divisions. Avoid placing all Elite and Strong teams in one division — it creates an unbalanced schedule advantage.

Tip 2 — Two-team tiers (Elite/Basement) work best at even numbers. One per conference keeps symmetry in title races and relegation battles.

Tip 3 — Use random assignment. Roll 1d5 (Elite=1, Strong=2, Average=3, Weak=4, Basement=5), re-rolling if a tier is full. This prevents you unconsciously biasing your favourite team.

Close Game Factor (CGF) Results

Used when the final regulation margin is ≤8 points. The trailing team gets a possession attempt modified by the CGF Roll.

How to Use the CGF Chart

Step 1: Roll 2d6.
Step 2: Add the trailing team's CGF rating.
Step 3: Subtract the leading team's CGF rating.
Step 4: Look up the modified total (range 2–28) in the table below.
Step 5: Use the correct column based on the deficit:
    — Tied or losing by 1–3 → Column A
    — Losing by 4–8 → Column B
    — Leading team's CGF is higher → Column C (opponent counter-attempt chance)

Fantasy Draft Mode

Don't want to roll entirely new teams? Use the historical team database as a draft pool. Over 100 years of real NFL, AFL, USFL, and other league data is available — all pre-rated and ready to play.

What Is Fantasy Draft Mode?

Fantasy Draft Mode lets you build a custom season using historical teams as your roster. You can mix eras — pit the 1994 Cowboys against the 1978 Steelers — or build a league from a single season. Each team's OFF, DEF, and CGF ratings reflect their historical performance, already calibrated for Quick Play Football.

How to Run a Fantasy Draft

Step 1 — Open the Season Browser
Open fantasy-season.html in your browser. Browse available seasons by league and year.
Step 2 — Pick Your Pool
Select a league or season as your draft pool. The browser shows each team's name, abbreviation, OFF, DEF, and CGF ratings.
Step 3 — Draft or Auto-Assign
Snake draft: Managers take turns picking teams in alternating order.
Random: Shuffle all teams and deal them out equally.
Auction: Each manager gets 100 points to bid on teams.
Step 4 — Import to Simulator
Open index.html and select teams from the loaded dataset to play matchups. Or use the Team Creator (teamcreator.html) to export your drafted league as a custom dataset.

Mix-Era Rules

When mixing teams from different eras, use the following optional adjustments to keep games competitive:

Era Period Modifier Suggestion
Modern2000–2015No modifier — use as rated
Mid-era1975–1999+1 DEF (defensive football era)
Classic1946–1974−1 OFF, +1 CGF (ground game era)
All-Time Fantasy League: For the definitive time-travel league, take the best team from each decade (1950s, 60s, 70s, 80s, 90s, 00s, 10s) and run a 7-team round-robin to find the greatest team in history.
